2026-04-28 (TUESDAY): FAKE CLOUD STORAGE FULL EMAILS

AUTHOR(S):

- Reethika Ramesh

Notes:
- We have discovered emails that falsely warn recipients that their mailbox storage limit has been exceeded. These emails contain links, often using shorteners, that redirect to fraudulent “Cloud” storage pages.
- The emails create a sense of immediate threat using urgent and fear inducing language such as “Cloud storage is full”, "Permanent data loss warning" and "Cloud Services Disabled".
- It pressures the recipient to follow their prompts, which takes them through several redirect chains and lands on pages designed to look like Cloud storage service dashboards. 
- These pages mimic OS specific details such as having the “Activate Windows” watermark. The pop-ups on the page are designed to look like they are system dialog boxes that occupy the user’s whole screen even though they are browser based.
- If a user follows the prompts on the page, it redirects them to websites to purchase VPN services or antivirus software.
- These malicious emails originate from email addresses associated with different firebaseapp websites and feature noreply in the username.
