2025-06-25 (THURSDAY): PHISHING CAMPAIGN IMPERSONATING MICROSOFT LOGIN PAGES

AUTHOR:

- Ruian Duan, Shu Wang

NOTES:

- We've seen over 500 newly registered domains (NRDs) in a phishing campaign impersonating Microsoft login pages. 
- Attackers are continuously registering the domains in batches.
- The root domain contains 10 characters and uses a top-level domain (TLD) of .shop.
- The fully qualified domain name (FQDN) includes a 10-character subdomain as a prefix.
- These domain names are likely created through a domain generation algorithm (DGA).
- The characteristics of a domain's infrastructure (nameserver) will change for phishing attacks after registration.

EXAMPLE OF PHISHING INFRASTRUCTURE (NAMESERVER) FOR A DOMAIN JUST AFTER REGISTRATION:

- poructablo[.]shop  NS ns1.nsdjj[.]xyz
- poructablo[.]shop  NS ns2.nsdjj[.]xyz
- ns1.nsdjj[.]xyz     A 84.200.17[.]195
- ns2.nsdjj[.]xyz     A 84.200.17[.]195
- www.nsdjj[.]xyz     A 84.32.84[.]32 

EXAMPLE NAMESERVER SERVER IP ADDRESS WHEN PERFORMING THE PHISHING ACTIVITY:

- poructablo[.]shop           21600 IN  NS  ns2.maindksdns[.]com
- poructablo[.]shop           21600 IN  NS  ns1.maindksdns[.]com
- aab4hjsehl.poructablo[.]shop  300 IN  A   162.0.233[.]153

- This campaign shares the same characteristics of an AiTM attack reported in Feburary 2025:
  -- https://www.invictus-ir.com/news/locked-out-dropboxed-in-when-bec-threats-innovate
- Domains from the Feb 2025 activity share the same nameserver of ns*.maindksdns[.]com
- The domains from Feb 2025 also follow similar naming patterns.
