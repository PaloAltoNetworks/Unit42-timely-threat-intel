2026-05-01 (FRIDAY): WebSocket Backdoor Campaign Injecting Credit Card Skimmers

AUTHOR(S):

- Billy Melicher, Nabeel Mohamed, Alex Starov

NOTES:

- Obfuscated JavaScript creates a WebSocket backdoor using dynamically executed JavaScript. 
- The WebSocket sends an obfuscated JavaScript payload to inject a credit card skimmer into the webpage.
- When entered, credit card information is sent back over the WebSocket to the attacker’s domain.

DETAILS: 

- The campaign uses a combination of shared dedicated infrastructure as well as CF proxies. For example, the C2 domains lgstd[.]net, lgstd[.]ws, and stores-content[.]com are hosted on the dedicated server 45.227.255[.]221 and the C2 domains logstash[.]in, logstorage[.]in, siteimproveanalytic[.]net are hosted on the dedicated server 5.188.86[.]202 whereas the C2 domains astryxrix[.]com, iconstaff[.]top, nexorxwa[.]com are using CF proxies. 
- Monitoring dedicated IPs for new hosting may assist in uncovering new attacks belonging to the same campaign.
- The campaign uses a few distinct masquerading themes to further evade detection. 
--  They include JS frameworks (e.g. jqueryuslibs[.]com, jqueurystatic[.]xyz), CDN/statics (e.g. frontstatics[.]com, api-statics[.]com), Monitoring/analytics (e.g. js-agentnewrelic[.]com, hexaqon-analytics[.]com) , Site Infra/Support (e.g. sitesupport[.]vip, store-content[.]net), and Brand typosquatting (e.g. gstatlc[.]org impersonating gstatic, js-agentnewrelic[.]com impersonating New Relic).
- We observed that domains are being registered in mini-batches on the same day, spanning across months. For example, logstash[.]in and logstorage[.]in were registered on 2025-02-19, and qetbootstrap[.]com, bootstrapcda[.]com and sitesocket[.]store were registered on 2025-05-01. 
- Additionally, they exhibited similar NS fingerprints. Monitoring for bulk registrations with similar fingerprints would help to proactively uncover additional C2 domains in the campaign.
