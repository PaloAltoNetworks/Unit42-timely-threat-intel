2026-03-30: KIMWOLF V7 IoT BOTNET EVOLUTION

AUTHOR(S): Asher Davila Loranca, Chris Navarrete, Doel Santos

NOTES:

- We identified a new version of the Kimwolf Android botnet (v7) that introduces enhancements to both DDoS attack capabilities and command-and-control (C2) infrastructure resilience. 
- Kimwolf v7 evolves from previous versions with new capabilities, including HTTP/2-based DDoS floods, Tor hidden service backup C2, and multiple Ethereum RPC endpoints for decentralized C2 resolution.


DETAILS:

- In this Kimwolf variant, we observed several notable upgrades to its C2 infrastructure and attack capabilities. 
- For C2, the malware includes 5 hardcoded public Ethereum RPC endpoints, used to query the blockchain for ENS (Ethereum Name Service) domain resolution to obtain the actual C2 IP address. 

- A hardcoded v3 Tor .onion address present in this malware provides a backup C2 channel via a SOCKS5 proxy state machine, which activates when primary ENS resolution fails. 
- All C2 traffic routes through a local proxy at 127[.]0.0[.]1:23075 before reaching the external C2 server, a modular design that allows the proxy component to be updated independently and provides flexibility to switch between clearnet and Tor routing. 
- Its dispatch table supports 15 DDoS methods, including TCP SYN/ACK/RST floods, multiple UDP flood variants, DNS query floods, ICMP floods, an epoll-based TCP connection flood, a TLS/HTTPS flood , and a game server UDP flood targeting port 27015. 
-- The most significant addition is an HTTP/2 flood. 
- This attack constructs complete Chrome browser fingerprints, including sec-ch-ua, sec-ch-ua-mobile, sec-ch-ua-platform, sec-fetch-site, sec-fetch-mode, sec-fetch-user, sec-fetch-dest, and priority: u=0, i headers, with dynamically generated user-agent strings, making the flood traffic difficult to distinguish from legitimate browser requests. 
- A high-performance UDP flood variant uses a Xorshift256 PRNG, with ARM NEON SIMD instructions accelerating IP/UDP checksum computation to maximize packet throughput.

ADDITIONAL DETAILS:

- The Kimwolf botnet (also tracked as AISURU) has been active since August 2024 and targets Android TV boxes, primarily cheap uncertified smart TVs and set-top boxes. 
- It monetizes through DDoS-for-hire services, residential proxy bandwidth sales via ByteConnect SDK, and app installation revenue.
- v7 was discovered on February 3, 2026, through threat hunting activities following public disclosures by XLab, Synthient, Infoblox, and KrebsOnSecurity in December 2025 through January 2026. 
- The development of v7, with its enhanced infrastructure resilience, directly responds to takedown efforts during that period.
- Organizations should monitor Ethereum RPC traffic and Tor circuit establishment from IoT and Android devices, which are strong behavioral indicators of Kimwolf infection.
