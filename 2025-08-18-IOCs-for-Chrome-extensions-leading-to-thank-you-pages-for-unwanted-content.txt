NOTES:

- A new Chrome extension campaign abuses the extension's onInstalled event to open post-install "thank you" pages.
- These "thank you" pages secretly load iframes from suspicious domains, exposing users to adware and Potentially Unwanted Programs (PUPs).

DETAILS:

- Campaign Overview: Dozens of Chrome extensions (60+ observed) are using their install pages to embed hidden iframes.
- When the browser triggers the onInstalled event after installation, the extension opens a thankyou.html or similar page.
- Embedded iframes in these "thank you" pages load content from suspicious URLs via such as:
  -- hxxps[:]//newextensioninstallweb[.]com/
  -- hxxps[:]//extensioninstallnotifer[.]com/
  -- hxxps[:]//xuix[.]top/qrcode-verifychrome-extension
- These iframes fetch content from suspicious third-party domains, often delivering adware or PUPs.
- Some of these extensions have a "Featured" flag, high ratings and reviews on the Chrome Web Store.
- Some of these extensions have been available for more than a year.
