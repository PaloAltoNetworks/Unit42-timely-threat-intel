2026-03-19 (THURSDAY): THE "GHOST IN THE IT" CAMPAIGN: SIGNED RMM & WORD-MASHUP DOMAIN

AUTHOR(S):

- Jingwen Shi, Zong-Yu Wu, Shu Wang, Tyler Halfpop

NOTES:

- A new malicious campaign is leveraging digitally signed, remote access software to bypass detection and maintain a stealthy, persistent foothold in target networks. 

- The attack triggers a popular file-hosting service's updater to launch its associated cloud-syncing executable whenever the host is online, facilitating traffic masquerading and potential data exfiltration.

- A significant portion of the attack infrastructure uses word-mashup domains—randomly combined English words (e.g., strongfitdealsforman[.]top) designed to appear benign to human eyes and bypass basic heuristic filters.

- Timeline: The campaign maintained a low-level baseline of activity starting in July 2024, followed by a rapid surge in detections beginning in August 2025.

- Scale: Identified 340 related samples and 224 unique domains/IP addresses associated with this campaign.


DETAILS:

- Signed RMM (Remote Monitoring and Management) Hijacking: Exploits the legitimate Remote Access tool.
  -- Leverages valid digital signatures to bypass reputation-based security filters and EDR (Endpoint Detection and Response) alerts.

- Word-Mashup Infrastructure: Employs semantically plausible English combinations for C2 domains (e.g., greenlandsforcowfoods[.]top, mensshoppinguputfitsdeals[.]shop).
  -- Uses low-cost TLDs (.top, .info, .shop) for rapid, disposable failover to bypass DGA (Domain Generation Algorithm) detection.

- Process Tree Decoupling via Legitimate Proxies: Instead of spawning the content-sharing service directly, the attack triggers a trusted updater binary to initiate the process. This orphans the malicious activity from the primary RMM process, effectively defeating heuristic monitoring.
  -- Defeats heuristic monitoring by isolating malicious operations from the primary RMM "Remote Access.exe" process.
  -- SaaS Traffic Masquerading: Abuses the popular content sharing service to generate high-volume HTTPS noise to a major cloud service provider (CSP). Uses /silentConfig to hide unauthorized data synchronization within routine cloud traffic.

- Aggressive Hardening & Persistence: Automates system-wide permission and network changes to maintain access.
  -- Manipulates native firewall rules via netsh to permit remote IP ingress and uses icacls to grant Users group permanent execution rights.
